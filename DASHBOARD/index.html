The Bhagavad Gita mainly aims to guide human beings on how to live a meaningful and righteous life. It presents a conversation between Krishna and Arjuna during the war described in the Mahabharata. When Arjuna becomes confused and unwilling to fight in the battle of Kurukshetra, Krishna teaches him about duty (dharma), righteousness, and the true nature of life. The text emphasizes that every individual must perform their responsibilities with honesty and courage. Another key aim of the Bhagavad Gita is to teach the importance of selfless action, spiritual wisdom, and devotion. It explains that people should perform their duties without being attached to the results of their actions, as attachment leads to stress and suffering. The Gita also highlights that the soul is eternal and that true peace comes from self-discipline, knowledge, and devotion to the divine. Overall, its message encourages individuals to maintain balance in life while following the path of duty, morality, and spiritual growth.
